About Lahar

A city where Mahabharata legends live on in every stone and every soul.

Lahar is a vibrant Nagar Palika city nestled in the Bhind District of Madhya Pradesh, India. Positioned along the fertile plains near the majestic Chambal River, this historic city carries the weight of millennia on its shoulders — tracing its origins all the way back to the Mahabharata era.

Local legend holds that Lakchhagrih — the fabled palace built of lac by the Kauravas for the Pandavas — stood in this very region. When fire consumed the palace, the Pandavas escaped through an underground tunnel leading to the nearby village of Nadigaun, a tale still whispered among Lahar's elders.

Through the medieval period, Lahar flourished under the influence of regional rulers of the Chambal belt, evolving as a significant center of trade and local administration. The city is also proudly known as the ancestral home of celebrated Hindi poet Hari Babu Gandhi, whose literary legacy continues to inspire.

Today, Lahar thrives as a tehsil headquarters, a hub for agriculture — wheat, rice, mustard, and pulses — and small-scale industries including handloom weaving and brick-making. Its markets serve dozens of surrounding villages, and its culture pulses with the rich traditions of the Chambal division.

❖

The city experiences a subtropical climate with blazing summers (35–45°C), a refreshing monsoon from June to September, and cool winters (8–25°C). Lahar's spirit, however, remains warm in every season — welcoming, proud, and rooted in tradition.

Ambrish Guddu Sharma
MLA · Lahar Assembly Constituency

Biography

Shri Ambrish Sharma, popularly known as "Guddu Sharma", is the elected Member of Legislative Assembly (MLA) from the Lahar Assembly Constituency in Madhya Pradesh. He is known for ground-level connect and public-focused development efforts.

His leadership focuses on infrastructure, farmer support, youth opportunities, and civic services across Lahar and surrounding villages in Bhind district.

Key Achievements & Contributions

  • Road connectivity and local infrastructure upgrades in Lahar tehsil.
  • Support for education facilities and youth-focused initiatives.
  • Advocacy for farmer welfare, irrigation access, and rural development.
  • Promotion of public services including water, electricity, and civic amenities.
  • Support for sports development through district-level facilities and events.
